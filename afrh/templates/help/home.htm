What is in This Database?

Though the Cane River Heritage Inventory & Map has been created as a database for historic resources in the Cane River area, it is really much more. Not only can physical heritage be recorded, like plantation homes or graves, but the database will also hold records for people or organizations, digital files for photos or audio interviews, and management activities like an archaeological survey or preservation intervention.

The database places resources into six basic categories:

  • Historic Resource physical resources (buildings, graves, statues)
  • Historic Area areas of historic or cultural importance
  • Activity resource management or documentation activities
  • Historic Event significant events of the past
  • Actors individuals or organizations
  • Information Resource any type of document, whether digital or physical

These categories each hold different sets of information, and resources in any category may be related to those in any other category. Furthermore, any resource may include spatial coordinates, to give it a location on the earth. That's where the MAP comes in, a way to view the resources spatially as they exist in the real world. To enhance the map, we've added a number of overlays made from historic maps of the area--some of which have never been published before!
